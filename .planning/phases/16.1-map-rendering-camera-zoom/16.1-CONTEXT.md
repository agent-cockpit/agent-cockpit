<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Camera Zoom
- Fixed 2x zoom — 32px source tiles render at 64px on screen. No user-controlled zoom toggle.
- Player is always centered in the viewport (standard RPG camera follow)
- Camera continues using existing LERP follow from Camera.ts
- Camera clamped to map bounds — camera never shows black space beyond map edges
- At 2x zoom, the effective world is 6464×6464 screen pixels; bounds clamping applies in zoomed space

### Claude's Discretion
- Tilemap rendering strategy (pre-render to offscreen canvas once at load vs dynamic per-frame tile lookup — pre-render strongly recommended for 60fps with 10k+ tiles)
- Wang tile index decoding and tileset PNG selection
- Object rendering order (use `layer` field from objects/manifest.json for draw order)
- Player starting position in new 3232×3232 world
- Overlay (overlay-0.png, world coords x=128 y=96, 672×832px) rendering — blit as canvas layer
- Updating `WORLD_W`/`WORLD_H` constants in GameState.ts

</decisions>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- The zoom factor (2x) means `ctx.scale(2, 2)` wrapping the entire world render — tiles, objects, NPCs, player all scale together. Camera x/y target should be computed in world-space then the scale handles screen projection.
- Tilemap render is performance-critical. Pre-rendering the full 3232×3232 map to a single offscreen canvas (or OffscreenCanvas) at load time and then blitting the visible viewport slice is the recommended approach over drawing ~10k tiles per frame.
- The 30 objects from objects/manifest.json have `boundingBox.x/y` in world-pixel coordinates (already in the 3232×3232 space based on the export). Render them using `layer` order after terrain, before NPC sprites.

</specifics>
